About

Expmotor is a web recruitment tool used to register participants for experiments run at the Centre for Experimental Research on Fairness, Inequality and Rationality, FAIR at NHH.

Expmotor was developed for the implementation of the international experiment "mmtk" in the spring of 2007. Back then, it became relevant to invite possibly the entire Faculty of Social Sciences at the University of Oslo, and it would be too much for one individual to maintain email correspondence with so many. Erik Sørensen therefore hacked a simple interface to a database that enabled participants to register for sessions without interference from the research group. The first version of Expmotor was coded in PHP. This system worked, but it was somewhat primitive. It was used a couple of times at the Norwegian School of Economics (NHH), but also by Sigve Tjøtta and Gaute Torsvik at the University of Bergen (UiB).

In the summer of 2009, Trond Halvorsen needed a small project to get into Python/Django programming. So he and Erik started a redesign of Expmotor for Python/Django. This was completed over the summer and has been in use for various experiments. Sigve Tjøtta has adopted this version, and Sandvik/Kobbeltvedt at NHH have also used Expmotor outside the core group.

The current version of Expmotor was rewritten from scratch and given a modernized look in 2021 by Sebastian Fest. It has been used since for many more projects by the research group but also externals such as Joel Berge and Aysil Emirmahmutoglu. It is currently maintained by the Data Infrastrcuture Unit at FAIR.

If you wish to use expmotor as an administrator to recruit participants for your experiment, you may open up an account. We will usually approve your request within 48 hours.
